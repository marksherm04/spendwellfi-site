Questions, answered honestly

How does it get my transactions without connecting to my bank?

You download statements (PDF or CSV) from your bank's website — the same files you can already access — and drop them into SpendWellFi. The app parses them locally. It's one extra step compared to bank-linking apps, and it's the step that means nobody else ever holds your data.

Which banks are supported?

PDF statements from Chase (checking, business, credit cards), PNC (checking, credit cards), Bank of America, Wells Fargo, American Express, Discover, and Betterment, plus CSV imports from dozens more. Unrecognized formats fall back to a generic parser, and we add banks continually — if yours doesn't work, send us a redacted sample and we'll usually support it within a release.

What happens to my data if I stop using the app?

It's a SQLite database file in your Documents folder. It's yours — export to CSV/Excel anytime, or open the file with any SQLite tool. No lock-in is possible because we never had your data to begin with.

Is it really one-time? What about updates?

Yes — $49 once, and all version 1 updates are free. If we someday ship a major version 2 with substantial new features, upgrading will be optional and discounted; version 1 will keep working regardless.

Windows says "unrecognized app" when installing — is it safe?

Our installers are code-signed by Sherman Solutions LLC. Brand-new releases can still show Windows SmartScreen's caution screen until Microsoft's reputation system catches up — click "More info → Run anyway." The download from this site is always the authentic build.
