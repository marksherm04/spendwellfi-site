A different deal with your data

Most finance apps work by holding your bank credentials and copying every transaction to their servers. That copy is what gets analyzed, sold, breached, or fed to AI training. SpendWellFi inverts the model: you keep the data, and the app comes to you.

01

What people ask before buying

How do my transactions get in without a bank login?

You sign in to your bank's website like normal and download a statement (PDF or CSV). Drop it into SpendWellFi. The parsing happens on your computer. It's the one extra step that means nobody else holds your data — and most people forget it's an extra step within a week.

Will it work with my bank?

PDFs from Chase (personal, business, credit cards), PNC checking and credit cards, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, American Express, Discover, and Betterment are all directly supported. CSV imports cover dozens more. Unknown formats fall through to a generic parser. If your bank doesn't work, send a redacted sample to support@spendwellfi.com and there's a good chance it'll be supported in the next release.

What if SpendWellFi goes away someday?

Your database is a plain SQLite file in your Documents folder. The app keeps working without me. You can open the file with any SQLite tool, export to CSV or Excel anytime, and your existing copy of the app keeps running because nothing about it phones home. This is the part subscription apps can't promise.
